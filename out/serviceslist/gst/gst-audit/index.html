What is GST Audit?

GST audit is a systematic examination of GST returns, invoices, books of accounts and tax payments to verify whether a taxpayer has correctly reported turnover, claimed input tax credit (ITC), and paid applicable GST as per law.

The objective of GST audit is to ensure compliance and detect discrepancies, under-reporting or incorrect ITC claims.

Types of GST Audit

1. Departmental Audit

Conducted by GST officers under Section 65 of GST Act. Officers examine records at business premises or office.

2. Special Audit

Conducted under Section 66 when authorities believe that value of supply or ITC claimed is not within normal limits. Audit is conducted by Chartered Accountant appointed by department.

GST Audit Process

  1. Issue of audit notice.
  2. Submission of documents and records.
  3. Verification & reconciliation.
  4. Audit report preparation.
  5. Final findings & demand (if any).

Documents Required for GST Audit

  • GST returns (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, GSTR-9)
  • Sales & purchase register
  • Input tax credit reconciliation
  • Financial statements
  • E-way bills and invoices

Risks of Non-Compliance

If discrepancies are found, the department may issue demand notices, impose penalties, or initiate legal proceedings. Proper audit assistance ensures correct representation and minimizes exposure.
