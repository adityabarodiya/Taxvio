What is GST Cancellation?

GST cancellation is the process of legally closing or surrendering your GST registration when your business is discontinued, merged, transferred, or no longer required to be registered under GST. Once cancelled, the taxpayer is no longer required to file GST returns.

Types of GST Cancellation

  • Voluntary Cancellation: When business is closed or turnover falls below threshold.
  • Compulsory Cancellation: Initiated by GST officer due to non-compliance or fraud.
  • Cancellation Due to Transfer/Merger: In case of business restructuring.

GST Cancellation Process

  1. Filing cancellation application on GST portal (REG-16).
  2. Submission of required documents.
  3. Verification by GST officer.
  4. Issuance of cancellation order.

Documents Required for GST Cancellation

  • GST login credentials
  • Reason for cancellation
  • Business closure proof (if applicable)
  • Final return details (GSTR-10)

What is Revocation of GST Cancellation?

If GST registration is cancelled by the department due to non-filing of returns, the taxpayer can apply for revocation within prescribed time limits. Revocation restores the GSTIN, allowing business operations to resume legally.

GST Cancellation Timeline

  • Application submission: Immediate
  • Officer verification: 15–30 days
  • Final cancellation order: Within 30 days

GST Cancellation vs GST Revocation – Key Differences

BasisGST CancellationGST Revocation
MeaningPermanent closure or surrender of GST registration.Restoration of cancelled GST registration.
Who InitiatesTaxpayer (voluntary) or GST officer (compulsory).Taxpayer applies after cancellation by department.
ReasonBusiness closure, turnover below limit, merger or non-compliance.Cancellation due to non-filing of returns or compliance default.
Form UsedREG-16 (Application for Cancellation).REG-21 (Application for Revocation).
Time LimitCan be filed anytime when eligible.Must be filed within 30 days of cancellation order.
OutcomeGSTIN becomes inactive permanently.GSTIN is restored and business operations resume.

GST cancellation and GST revocation are often confused. Cancellation ends your GST registration, whereas revocation restores a cancelled registration. Choosing the correct process depends on your business status and compliance history. Professional guidance ensures that mistakes are avoided and future legal complications are prevented.
