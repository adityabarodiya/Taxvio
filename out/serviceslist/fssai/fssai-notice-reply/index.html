Understanding the Notice

What Is an FSSAI Notice and Why Must You Respond?

An FSSAI Notice is a formal official communication issued by the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India or a designated food safety officer when a food business operator is found to be in violation of — or suspected of violating — the provisions of the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006 or its associated regulations.

FSSAI notices are not informal warnings. They are legally binding communications that carry specific timelines for response, typically ranging from 7 to 30 days from the date of issuance. Failure to respond within the prescribed deadline is itself treated as non-compliance and can escalate consequences significantly — including automatic suspension or cancellation of your food license.

A notice may arise from a routine inspection, a consumer complaint, a discrepancy in your annual return filing, non-renewal of license, or findings during a market surveillance exercise. Regardless of the trigger, the response must be structured, evidence-backed, and legally compliant — a poorly written or inadequate reply can worsen your situation.

At Taxvio, our compliance and legal team has extensive experience handling all categories of FSSAI notices. We analyse the specific allegations, identify the strongest defence, compile supporting documentation, and draft a professionally worded reply that maximises the chances of a favourable outcome for your food business.

Notice Categories

Types of FSSAI Notices Taxvio Handles

FSSAI issues different types of notices depending on the severity of the violation. Each requires a distinct response strategy and documentation approach.

🔧

Improvement Notice

Low–Medium

Issued under Section 32 of the FSS Act when a food safety officer observes minor non-compliance during an inspection — such as hygiene deficiencies, labeling issues, or inadequate food handling practices. The FBO is given a specific period (usually 14–30 days) to rectify the deficiencies and submit proof of correction.

⚠️ If ignored: Non-response can escalate to a Show Cause or Suspension Notice.

✅ Taxvio's approach: Taxvio prepares a detailed compliance action plan, supporting photographs, rectification evidence, and a formal written reply to the food safety officer.

📄

Show Cause Notice

Medium–High

Issued when the authority has reason to believe that a food business has committed a significant violation and wants the FBO to explain why action — including penalty, suspension, or cancellation — should not be taken. A written response with supporting documents is mandatory within a stated timeline.

⚠️ If ignored: Inadequate response leads to ex-parte adverse orders against your license.

✅ Taxvio's approach: Taxvio drafts a legally structured show cause reply addressing each allegation point-by-point, backed by compliance records and legal provisions.

⛔

Suspension Notice

High

Issued when the food safety authority temporarily suspends a food license due to serious non-compliance findings, significant hygiene violations, or failure to comply with an earlier Improvement Notice. Business operations must cease immediately upon receiving this notice until compliance is demonstrated.

⚠️ If ignored: Continued operation after suspension notice constitutes a criminal offence.

✅ Taxvio's approach: Taxvio initiates urgent compliance rectification, prepares a reinstatement application, and coordinates directly with the food safety authority for expedited restoration.

❌

Cancellation Notice

Critical

The most serious FSSAI notice — issued for repeated violations, criminal offences under the FSS Act, or failure to comply with a suspension order. Cancellation of the FSSAI license means permanent closure of food business operations under that license. Legal representation and expert response are absolutely critical.

⚠️ If ignored: Permanent loss of food license and prohibition from food business operations.

✅ Taxvio's approach: Taxvio provides immediate legal and compliance consultation, prepares a comprehensive defence, and represents the FBO's interests through the formal adjudication process.

Why Notices Are Issued

Common Reasons for Receiving an FSSAI Notice

Understanding why the notice was issued is the first step toward an effective response. These are the most common triggers food safety officers cite in FSSAI notices.

🏷️

Improper Food Labeling

Missing FSSAI number, incorrect nutritional info, absent allergen declarations, or non-compliant packaging.

🧹

Hygiene & Sanitation Issues

Poor cleanliness of premises, contaminated water supply, pest infestations, or unhygienic food handling.

📅

Expired or Lapsed License

Operating a food business with an expired or non-renewed FSSAI license detected during inspection.

📊

Non-Filing of Annual Returns

Failure to file mandatory Form D1/D2 annual returns by the May 31 deadline for State/Central license holders.

🍽️

Unauthorized Product Category

Manufacturing or selling food products not listed or approved under your current FSSAI license category.

👤

Consumer Complaints

Complaints about food quality, adulteration, foreign matter, or food poisoning incidents reported to FSSAI.

🔍

Market Surveillance Findings

Substandard or unsafe food products detected during random market sampling and laboratory testing.

🏭

Inspection Deficiencies

Non-compliance observed during scheduled or surprise inspections by food safety officers.

📋

Record-Keeping Failures

Absence of food safety management records, temperature logs, or batch production documentation.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions — FSSAI Notice Reply

What should I do immediately after receiving an FSSAI notice?+
Read the notice carefully to understand the specific allegations and the response deadline stated on it. Do not ignore it or delay. Contact a compliance professional like Taxvio immediately — early engagement gives us more time to prepare a stronger reply and gather the right documentation.
What is the difference between an Improvement Notice and a Show Cause Notice?+
An Improvement Notice (Section 32) is issued for minor non-compliance and asks you to rectify specific deficiencies within a set period. A Show Cause Notice is more serious — it requires you to explain why penal action, including suspension or cancellation of license, should not be taken against you. Show Cause Notices demand a detailed, legally structured written response.
Can my FSSAI license be cancelled without warning?+
No. FSSAI follows a process of natural justice — you will receive a notice and an opportunity to respond before cancellation. However, in cases of severe violations or criminal offences under the FSS Act, emergency suspension can happen quickly. This is why responding promptly to every earlier-stage notice is critical.
Can Taxvio attend the FSSAI hearing on my behalf?+
Yes. Taxvio prepares you thoroughly for any scheduled personal hearing before the food safety authority and can accompany you or represent your interests during the hearing. We prepare key arguments, supporting documents, and responses to anticipated questions.
How long does it take to resolve an FSSAI notice?+
Resolution timelines vary by notice type and the authority's processing schedule. Improvement notices can often be closed within 30–45 days with proper documentation. Show cause and suspension matters may take 60–120 days. Taxvio follows up proactively at every stage to avoid unnecessary delays.
Will fixing the issues mentioned in the notice guarantee it is closed?+
Not automatically. You must submit formal written proof of rectification along with your notice reply. Simply fixing the problem without informing the authority does not result in notice closure. Taxvio ensures that all corrective actions are properly documented, photographed where needed, and formally submitted as part of your reply.
Can a cancelled FSSAI license be restored?+
Restoration of a cancelled FSSAI license is a complex legal process and is not guaranteed. In some cases, cancellation orders can be appealed before the Food Safety Appellate Tribunal or the High Court. However, the best strategy is always to respond to earlier-stage notices effectively and avoid reaching the cancellation stage.
