Complete EMI Guide

Everything You Need to Know About EMI

A complete guide to understanding Equated Monthly Instalments, amortization, and smart loan repayment strategies in India.

What Is an EMI & How Is It Calculated?

An Equated Monthly Instalment (EMI) is the fixed amount a borrower pays to the lender every month until the loan is fully repaid. Each EMI has two components — the principal repayment and the interest charge. The ratio of these two components changes every month — in the early months, the interest portion is higher, while in later months, more of the EMI goes toward reducing the principal. This is called the reducing-balance method, the standard used by all banks and RBI-regulated NBFCs in India.

Our EMI Calculator uses the standard formula: EMI = [P × r × (1+r)ⁿ] / [(1+r)ⁿ − 1] where P is the principal, r is the monthly rate, and n is the tenure in months — giving you results identical to your bank's loan offer letter.

7 Smart Tips to Reduce Your Total Loan Cost

01

Maximise Your Down Payment

A larger down payment directly reduces your principal and all future interest. For a home loan, a 20–30% down payment can save lakhs in total interest.

02

Negotiate Your Interest Rate

Your CIBIL score is your biggest bargaining chip. A score above 750 qualifies you for the lowest rates. Even 0.5% lower rate on ₹50L for 20 years saves ₹5–7 lakh.

03

Choose Optimal Tenure

Don't always pick the longest tenure just for a lower EMI. Use this calculator to find the sweet spot where EMI is affordable but total interest is minimized.

04

Make Annual Prepayments

Prepay using your annual bonus or windfall income. Prepaying ₹1 lakh extra in Year 1 on a ₹50L, 20-year loan saves ~₹3.5L in total interest.

05

Consider Balance Transfer

If RBI cuts repo rates and new loan rates fall, consider shifting your home loan to a lender offering a lower rate. Even 1% reduction can save significant interest.

06

Claim All Tax Benefits

Home loan borrowers can save up to ₹1.05L/year in taxes: ₹1.5L principal under 80C (30% slab = ₹45K) + ₹2L interest under 24B (30% slab = ₹60K).

07

Avoid Multiple Loans Simultaneously

Multiple EMIs strain cash flow and reduce eligibility for future credit. Pay off high-interest personal/car loans before taking a home loan.

08

Set Up Auto-Debit for EMI

Missing an EMI damages your CIBIL score and incurs late payment penalties. Set up an ECS/NACH auto-debit mandate to ensure on-time payment every month.
