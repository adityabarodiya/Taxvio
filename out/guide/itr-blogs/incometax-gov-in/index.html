Income Tax Portal Guide

What is Incometax.gov.in?

incometax.gov.in is the official website and e-filing portal of the Income Tax Department of India, operated under the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT), Ministry of Finance, Government of India. Launched in its current form in June 2021, it replaced the older portal (incometaxindiaefiling.gov.in) and serves as the single unified platform for all direct tax compliance and services in India.

The portal caters to more than 9 crore registered taxpayers — individuals, Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs), firms, companies, trusts, non-residents and government entities. It enables taxpayers to file Income Tax Returns (ITR), view tax credit statements (Form 26AS, AIS, TIS), check refund status, pay taxes via challans, respond to notices, manage PAN card services, link PAN-Aadhaar and access various other tax-related services — all online, 24×7.

For businesses and employers, the portal also facilitates TAN registration, TDS/TCS return filing, TDS certificate generation and compliance management. Tax professionals (Chartered Accountants, Enrolled Agents, Tax Return Preparers) can manage multiple client accounts through authorized access. The portal integrates with the TRACES (TDS Reconciliation Analysis and Correction Enabling System) portal for TDS credit tracking and Form 16/16A download.

Real-Time Processing

Always verify the official URL

The only official income tax portal URL is www.incometax.gov.in. Beware of phishing websites with similar-looking URLs (e.g., incometax.org.in, incometax-india.com). Always check for the HTTPS lock icon in your browser and never share your password or OTP with anyone.

What you can do

Key Services Available on Incometax.gov.in

The portal offers a comprehensive suite of services covering every aspect of income tax compliance and taxpayer support:

e-Filing of Income Tax Returns (ITR)

File ITR-1 to ITR-7 online for individuals, HUFs, firms, companies and trusts. Pre-filled data from AIS/TIS, auto-calculation of tax and instant acknowledgement (ITR-V).

PAN Card Services

Apply for new PAN (Form 49A/49AA), request PAN correction, reprint PAN card, link PAN with Aadhaar, verify PAN status and download e-PAN instantly.

Refund Status & Tracking

Check real-time status of ITR refund — whether it's under processing, issued to bank, or failed. Download refund confirmation and update bank account details.

Tax Payment via Challan

Pay advance tax, self-assessment tax, TDS/TCS, regular assessment tax and penalties using Challan 280 or ITNS 281 via net banking, debit/credit card or NEFT/RTGS.

View Form 26AS & TDS Certificate

Access your consolidated tax credit statement showing TDS deducted, advance tax paid, TCS collected and tax refunds. Download TDS certificates (Form 16/16A) from deductors.

Annual Information Statement (AIS)

View comprehensive income data reported by third parties — salary, interest, dividends, securities transactions, property sale, foreign remittance and high-value transactions.

Tax Information Statement (TIS)

Edit and reconcile AIS data before ITR filing. Accept, reject or provide feedback on income entries. Accepted entries auto-fill your ITR form.

Response to Notices & Orders

View and respond to notices under Section 143(2), 142(1), 148, 156 and others. Upload supporting documents, file rectification requests and track case status.

Outstanding Tax Demand

Check pending tax demands under Section 143(1), pay online via challan or file rectification if the demand is incorrect. View demand register and challan history.

Compliance & Pending Actions

Dashboard showing all pending items — unfiled ITRs, unverified returns, response pending to notices, outstanding demands, TDS defaults and other compliance alerts.

TAN Services for Deductors

Apply for TAN, file quarterly TDS/TCS returns (24Q, 26Q, 27Q, 27EQ), view challan status, download TDS certificates, file correction statements via TRACES.

Grievance Redressal & Help

Raise service requests for refund reissue, login issues, PAN-Aadhaar linking failures, rectification delays and other technical or procedural grievances. Track grievance status.

Getting started

How to Register on Incometax.gov.in

First-time users must register on the portal to access any service. Registration is mandatory even for viewing Form 26AS or checking refund status. Here is the complete registration process:

01

Visit incometax.gov.in

Open the official portal at www.incometax.gov.in in any browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge). Click on 'Register Yourself' or 'Register' link on the homepage.

02

Select user category

Choose 'Taxpayer' if you are an individual, HUF or AOP. Choose 'Tax Professional/CA' if you are an authorized representative. Companies and firms select 'Others'.

03

Enter PAN details

Enter your PAN (permanent account number), full name as per PAN, date of birth/date of incorporation and registered mobile number. Click 'Continue'.

04

Aadhaar OTP verification (if linked)

If your PAN is linked to Aadhaar, an OTP will be sent to your Aadhaar-registered mobile. Enter the 6-digit OTP to authenticate your identity.

05

Set password and security question

Create a strong password (min 8 characters with uppercase, lowercase, number and special character). Select a security question and provide an answer for account recovery.

06

Email and mobile verification

Enter your email address. OTPs will be sent to both email and mobile. Enter both OTPs to complete email/mobile verification and activate your account.

07

Complete profile details

Login with PAN and password. Navigate to 'My Profile' and add address, bank account (for refunds), communication preferences and other required details. Save the profile.

PAN is mandatory for registration

You cannot register on incometax.gov.in without a valid PAN. If you do not have PAN, apply for one using Form 49A (residents) or 49AA (foreign nationals) on the portal itself. e-PAN is issued instantly after verification.

Mobile and email verification required

Both mobile number and email address must be verified via OTP during registration. These are used for login alerts, ITR filing confirmations, refund notifications and important tax-related communications.

Access your account

Login Methods on Incometax.gov.in

The portal supports multiple secure login methods to accommodate different user preferences and security requirements:

🔑

User ID (PAN) + Password

Standard login method for all users. Enter your PAN as User ID and the password you set during registration. This is the most common login method.

Login steps:

  1. 1.Visit incometax.gov.in
  2. 2.Click 'Login'
  3. 3.Enter PAN and Password
  4. 4.Click 'Continue'
📱

Aadhaar OTP

Login using PAN and Aadhaar OTP if PAN-Aadhaar is linked. OTP is sent to Aadhaar-registered mobile. No password required. Valid for one session only.

Login steps:

  1. 1.Click 'Login with Aadhaar OTP'
  2. 2.Enter PAN
  3. 3.Click 'Request OTP'
  4. 4.Enter OTP received on Aadhaar mobile
🏦

EVC via Net Banking

Electronic Verification Code (EVC) generated via net banking. Available for select banks. Used primarily for ITR e-verification but can also be used for login.

Login steps:

  1. 1.Login to your bank's net banking
  2. 2.Generate EVC from tax section
  3. 3.Use EVC on income tax portal
  4. 4.Login or e-verify ITR
🔐

Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)

Companies, LLPs and businesses with Class 2/3 DSC can use digital signature for secure login. DSC is mandatory for certain business filings.

Login steps:

  1. 1.Connect DSC token/USB
  2. 2.Click 'Login with DSC'
  3. 3.Select certificate
  4. 4.Enter DSC password/PIN

File your return

How to File ITR on Incometax.gov.in

Filing your Income Tax Return on incometax.gov.in is streamlined with pre-filled data, auto-calculation and multiple e-verification options. Here is the complete process:

01

Login to incometax.gov.in

Login using your PAN and password or Aadhaar OTP. Navigate to the dashboard and click on 'e-File' > 'File Income Tax Return'.

02

Select assessment year and ITR form

Choose the assessment year (e.g., AY 2025-26 for FY 2024-25). The portal recommends the applicable ITR form based on your income sources, or you can select manually (ITR-1 to ITR-7).

03

Choose filing mode — Online or Upload

For ITR-1 and ITR-4, you can fill the form online (recommended for most users). For other ITR forms, you can prepare offline using ITR utility and upload JSON file.

04

Verify and edit pre-filled data

The portal pre-fills data from Form 26AS, AIS and TIS — salary, TDS, interest income, capital gains. Verify each section carefully and edit if required.

05

Enter additional income and deductions

Add any income not captured (business income, cash receipts, rental income). Claim deductions under Chapter VI-A (80C, 80D, 80G, etc.). Portal auto-calculates tax liability.

06

Pay self-assessment tax (if any)

If there is tax payable after TDS credit, pay via Challan 280 using net banking. Enter challan details (BSR code, date, serial number, amount) in the ITR before submitting.

07

Preview, validate and submit ITR

Preview the filled ITR. The portal validates for errors and missing mandatory fields. Resolve all errors, accept declaration and submit. Download ITR-V acknowledgement PDF.

08

E-verify ITR within 30 days

ITR must be e-verified within 30 days of filing. Use Aadhaar OTP, EVC via net banking, DSC, or send signed ITR-V by post to CPC Bangalore. Verification completes the filing process.

E-verification is mandatory within 30 days

Your ITR is not considered 'filed' until you e-verify it within 30 days of submission. If verification is missed, the ITR becomes invalid and you must file a fresh return. Use Aadhaar OTP (instant), EVC via net banking or send signed ITR-V by post to CPC Bangalore.

PAN management

PAN Card Services on Incometax.gov.in

The portal offers comprehensive PAN-related services — from new PAN application to correction, linking and verification:

Apply for New PAN Card

Individuals and entities without PAN can apply online using Form 49A (Indian residents) or Form 49AA (foreign nationals). Upload documents and receive e-PAN instantly after verification.

📍 Quick Links > Instant e-PAN

Request PAN Correction

Correct errors in PAN details — name, father's name, date of birth, address, photo, signature. Submit Form for Changes or Correction in PAN Data with supporting documents.

📍 Quick Links > Changes or Correction in PAN Data

Link PAN with Aadhaar

Mandatory linking as per Section 139AA. Enter PAN, Aadhaar number and name as per Aadhaar. OTP verification required. Free linking available on the portal.

📍 Quick Links > Link Aadhaar

Download e-PAN

Download e-PAN card (digitally signed PDF) instantly if you have applied for PAN online. Valid for all official purposes. No need to wait for physical card.

📍 Quick Links > Download e-PAN

Verify PAN Details

Verify whether a PAN is valid, active or deactivated. Check PAN details for name mismatch, date of birth and status. Used for vendor verification and KYC compliance.

📍 Quick Links > Verify Your PAN

Reprint/Reissue PAN Card

If PAN card is lost, damaged or incorrect details are printed, request reprint online. A new physical PAN card will be dispatched to your registered address.

📍 Services > Request for Reprint of PAN Card

PAN-Aadhaar linking is mandatory

As per Section 139AA, linking PAN with Aadhaar is mandatory for filing ITR from AY 2023-24. Non-linked PANs become inoperative from 1 July. Link for free on incometax.gov.in via Quick Links → Link Aadhaar. Verification via Aadhaar OTP is instant.

Track your refund

How to Check Income Tax Refund Status

You can check your ITR refund status both with and without login. Here are both methods:

🔐

With Login

  1. 1.Login to incometax.gov.in
  2. 2.Go to 'Services' > 'Refund/Demand Status'
  3. 3.Select Assessment Year
  4. 4.View refund status, amount and bank details
  5. 5.Download refund confirmation if issued
🔓

Without Login (PAN-based)

  1. 1.Visit incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal
  2. 2.Click 'Refund/Demand Status'
  3. 3.Enter PAN and Captcha
  4. 4.View refund status for all assessment years
  5. 5.Note: Cannot download confirmation without login

Understanding refund statuses:

Refund Determined

ITR processed; refund amount calculated and approved by CPC/AO.

➜ Wait for refund to be issued. Usually takes 7–21 days.

Refund Issued

Refund has been credited to your bank account via NEFT.

➜ Check bank statement. Download refund confirmation from portal.

Refund Failed

Refund could not be credited — bank account closed, name mismatch, incorrect IFSC, etc.

➜ Update bank account in 'My Profile'. Raise refund reissue request.

Refund Adjusted Against Demand

Refund was adjusted against outstanding tax demand from previous years.

➜ Check demand register. If incorrect, file rectification request.

Under Processing

ITR is being processed at CPC Bangalore. Refund not yet determined.

➜ Wait for processing to complete. Check after 2–4 weeks.

Pay taxes online

How to Pay Income Tax on Incometax.gov.in

You can pay all types of income tax online via challans. The portal supports net banking, debit/credit card and NEFT/RTGS payment modes. Here are the main challans:

Challan 280 — Income Tax Payment

Advance tax, self-assessment tax, regular assessment tax, tax on distributed income, and other direct tax payments by individuals, companies, firms.

Tax codes: 100 (Advance Tax), 102 (TDS/TCS), 300 (Self-Assessment Tax), 400 (Regular Assessment)

ITNS 281 — TDS/TCS Payment

Tax deducted at source (TDS) or tax collected at source (TCS) by deductors/collectors. Includes salary TDS, contractor TDS, professional fees TDS, rent TDS.

Tax codes: Section 192 (Salary), 194C (Contractor), 194J (Professional Fees), 194I (Rent)

Challan 282 — Gift Tax, Wealth Tax, Other Taxes

Now rarely used as gift tax and wealth tax have been abolished. May be used for securities transaction tax (STT) in specific cases.

Tax codes: Varies by tax type

How to pay tax via challan

Steps:

  1. 1. Login → e-Pay Tax → Select challan type (280/281)
  2. 2. Enter PAN, assessment year, tax type and amount
  3. 3. Choose payment mode (net banking/card)
  4. 4. Complete payment and download challan counterfoil
  5. 5. Note BSR code, date and serial number for ITR

Troubleshooting

Common Errors & Solutions on Incometax.gov.in

Here are the most frequently encountered issues and how to resolve them:

🔗

PAN-Aadhaar Not Linked

Solution: Go to Quick Links > Link Aadhaar. Enter PAN, Aadhaar and name. Verify via Aadhaar OTP. Linking is mandatory for ITR filing from AY 2023-24.

⚠ Impact: Cannot file ITR; PAN may become inoperative from 1 July

🔒

Forgot Password / Cannot Login

Solution: Click 'Forgot Password' on login page. Enter PAN. OTPs sent to registered email and mobile. Enter both OTPs and reset password. If email/mobile not accessible, raise service request.

⚠ Impact: Cannot access any portal service until password is reset

⚠️

Mismatch in Form 26AS and AIS

Solution: Download both Form 26AS and AIS. Reconcile manually. If income shown in AIS is incorrect, provide feedback in TIS. If TDS not reflected in 26AS, ask deductor to file correction return.

⚠ Impact: ITR processing delay, incorrect tax calculation, notices from ITD

💸

Refund Not Received / Refund Failed

Solution: Check refund status. If 'Refund Failed', update bank account in My Profile (name, account number, IFSC). Raise refund reissue request under Services > Service Request.

⚠ Impact: Refund stuck; must be manually reissued after bank update

⏰

ITR Not E-Verified Within 30 Days

Solution: If 30-day deadline missed, ITR is invalid. File a fresh return if still within due date, or file belated/revised return. Enable SMS alerts to avoid missing verification deadline in future.

⚠ Impact: ITR treated as not filed; penalties and interest applicable

📄

Outstanding Tax Demand — Incorrect Calculation

Solution: Login > e-File > Response to Outstanding Tax Demand. View demand order. If incorrect, file rectification request under Section 154 with supporting documents. Track rectification status online.

⚠ Impact: Demand interest accrues until rectified; may affect credit score

🔔

Notice Received — 143(2), 142(1), 148

Solution: View notice under e-File > Pending Actions or My Account > View Notices. Download notice PDF. Consult CA to draft response. Upload response and supporting documents before deadline. Track case status.

⚠ Impact: Non-compliance leads to best judgement assessment, penalties

Frequently asked questions

Incometax.gov.in FAQs

Q.What is the official website for income tax in India?

The official website is www.incometax.gov.in. This is the only authorized portal of the Income Tax Department of India for all tax-related services — ITR filing, PAN services, tax payment, refund tracking, notices, compliance and grievances. Do not use any third-party website claiming to be 'official' — always verify the URL is incometax.gov.in before entering any credentials.

Q.How do I register on incometax.gov.in for the first time?

Visit incometax.gov.in and click 'Register Yourself'. Enter your PAN, name, date of birth and mobile number. If PAN-Aadhaar is linked, verify using Aadhaar OTP. Set password and security question. Verify email and mobile via OTP. Complete profile with address and bank details. Registration is free and takes 10–15 minutes. Once registered, you can login anytime using PAN and password.

Q.Can I file ITR on incometax.gov.in without a CA?

Yes. Individual taxpayers with straightforward income sources (salary, interest, rental income, capital gains) can file ITR themselves using the online filing mode. ITR-1 (Sahaj) and ITR-4 (Sugam) have user-friendly online forms with pre-filled data and auto-calculation. For complex returns (business income, foreign assets, multiple properties, partnership), consulting a CA is recommended.

Q.How do I link my PAN with Aadhaar on incometax.gov.in?

Login to incometax.gov.in or visit without login via Quick Links > Link Aadhaar. Enter your PAN, Aadhaar number and name exactly as per Aadhaar card. Click 'Validate'. An OTP will be sent to your Aadhaar-registered mobile. Enter OTP and submit. Linking confirmation is displayed instantly. Linking is mandatory for ITR filing and to keep PAN active.

Q.What is the difference between Form 26AS, AIS and TIS?

Form 26AS is your tax credit statement showing TDS deducted, advance tax paid, TCS collected and refunds. AIS (Annual Information Statement) is a comprehensive data statement showing all income reported by third parties — salary, interest, dividends, securities transactions, property purchases, foreign remittance. TIS (Tax Information Statement) is an editable version of AIS where you can accept, reject or provide feedback on entries before using them in ITR. All three are available on incometax.gov.in.

Q.How can I check my income tax refund status?

Login to incometax.gov.in and go to Services > Refund/Demand Status. Select the assessment year and view refund status. You can also check without login by visiting incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal and entering your PAN. Status will show 'Refund Determined', 'Refund Issued', 'Refund Failed' or 'Under Processing'. If refund failed, update bank account and raise reissue request.

Q.Is incometax.gov.in safe for online transactions?

Yes. The portal uses 256-bit SSL encryption for all data transmission, multi-factor authentication (PAN + password/OTP/EVC/DSC) and secure payment gateways (SBI, HDFC, ICICI) for tax payments. All transactions are logged and can be audited. However, never share your password, OTP or login credentials with anyone. Always verify the URL is 'https://www.incometax.gov.in' before entering sensitive information.

Q.What should I do if I receive a notice on the portal?

Login to incometax.gov.in and go to e-File > Pending Actions or My Account > View Notices. Download the notice PDF and read it carefully. Check the section under which the notice is issued (143(2), 142(1), 148, 156, etc.) and the deadline for response. Consult a CA to understand the notice and prepare a response. Upload the response and supporting documents on the portal before the deadline. Track the case status under 'My Account'.

Disclaimer: This article is for general educational purposes. Portal features, login procedures, ITR forms and compliance requirements are subject to change through Finance Acts, CBDT notifications and departmental updates. Always verify current information on the official portal at incometax.gov.in or consult a qualified tax professional.
