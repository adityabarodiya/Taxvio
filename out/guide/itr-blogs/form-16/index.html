Income Tax Guide

What Is Form 16 and Who Needs It?

Form 16 is a TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) certificate issued by an employer to a salaried employee under Section 203 of the Income Tax Act, 1961. It officially confirms that the employer has deducted income tax from the employee's salary during the financial year and deposited the same with the Central Government on the employee's behalf.

Every salaried individual in India — whether a private sector employee, a government employee, a contractual worker receiving regular salary, or a director drawing remuneration from a company — should receive Form 16 from their employer if TDS has been deducted from their salary. Even if your income falls below the taxable threshold and no TDS was deducted, it is a good practice to request a nil-TDS acknowledgement or a letter confirming salary paid, since Form 16 is also commonly required for home loan applications, education loan processing, and certain visa applications.

The last date by which employers must issue Form 16 to employees is June 15 following the end of the financial year — for example, June 15, 2025 for FY 2024-25. Employers who fail to issue Form 16 by this deadline risk a penalty of ₹100 per day of default under Section 272A(2)(g) of the Income Tax Act.

Form 16 is not the same as Form 16A or Form 16B

Form 16 is specifically for salary income. Form 16A is issued by banks, clients and companies for non-salary TDS — such as interest, professional fees and rental income. Form 16B is issued by a property buyer to the seller for TDS under Section 194IA on property transactions. Always verify which form you need before filing your ITR.

Official TRACES document

Form 16 Part A — What It Contains

Part A of Form 16 is the officially government-generated component. It is downloaded by your employer directly from the TRACES (TDS Reconciliation Analysis and Correction Enabling System) portal and carries a unique TRACES certificate number that makes it tamper-evident. Part A primarily deals with the tax side of the salary relationship — confirming how much was deducted and deposited, quarter by quarter.

Employer Name & TAN

Legal name and Tax Deduction Account Number of the employer who deducted TDS from your salary.

Employee Name & PAN

Your full name and Permanent Account Number as registered with the employer for TDS purposes.

Period of Employment

The financial year period during which you were employed with this specific employer and TDS was deducted.

Summary of TDS Deposited

Quarter-wise breakup of salary paid and TDS deposited to the government — verified against TRACES records.

Challan Details

BSR code, challan serial number and date of TDS deposit for each quarter — matching the employer's TDS return.

TRACES Certificate Number

A unique certificate number generated by TRACES confirming the authenticity of Part A.

Verify authenticity using TRACES

Any Form 16 Part A that does not carry a TRACES watermark and unique certificate number should be treated as potentially invalid. Employees can verify the authenticity of their Form 16 Part A on the TRACES portal using the certificate number printed on the document.

Employer-prepared document

Form 16 Part B — What It Contains

Part B of Form 16 is prepared by the employer and details the complete salary computation — the income side of the TDS certificate. It shows how the employer arrived at the taxable salary figure, which deductions and exemptions were considered, and how the final TDS amount was calculated. Part B is the section you will spend the most time reviewing when preparing your ITR.

01

Gross Salary Breakup

Basic salary, House Rent Allowance (HRA), Leave Travel Allowance, special allowances, bonus and other salary components.

02

Exempt Allowances

HRA exemption computed under Section 10(13A), LTA exemption under Section 10(5), and other eligible exempt allowances under Section 10.

03

Standard Deduction

₹50,000 (old regime) or ₹75,000 (new regime) standard deduction applicable to salaried employees and pensioners.

04

Professional Tax

Professional tax deducted by the employer from salary and eligible as a deduction under Section 16(iii).

05

Income from Other Sources (Reported)

Interest from savings accounts or other income declared by the employee to the employer for consolidated TDS computation.

06

Chapter VI-A Deductions

Section 80C investments (PF, PPF, ELSS, LIC), Section 80D health insurance premiums, Section 80E education loan interest, and other eligible deductions.

07

Net Taxable Income

Gross salary minus exemptions and deductions — the income on which the employer computes the final tax liability.

08

Tax Payable & TDS Deducted

Total income tax payable on net taxable income and the actual TDS deducted and deposited during the year.

HRA calculation in Part B

The HRA exemption in Part B is based on what the employee declared to the employer. If you pay higher rent or did not submit actual rent receipts to HR, your employer may have under-claimed HRA. You can claim the correct HRA amount while filing your ITR directly, even if Form 16 shows a lower figure — subject to eligibility conditions.

Regime selection matters

Form 16 Part B reflects the tax regime your employer computed TDS under — new or old. If you declared your regime preference to the employer early in the year, Part B will reflect that. You can, however, switch regimes when filing your ITR, subject to applicable conditions.

Obtaining your document

How to Get Form 16 from Your Employer

Form 16 is issued by your employer — you cannot download it yourself from the income tax portal. The employer downloads Part A from TRACES after filing the annual TDS return (Form 24Q, Q4) and combines it with internally prepared Part B to create the complete Form 16. Here is the typical process:

01

Employer files annual TDS return

After the financial year ends, your employer files the fourth-quarter Form 24Q return with TDS details for all employees. This is typically due by May 31.

02

Part A downloaded from TRACES

Once the TDS return is processed, the employer logs in to TRACES and downloads Form 16 Part A for each employee. Part A carries a unique TRACES certificate number.

03

Part B prepared internally

The employer prepares Part B with the complete salary and deduction details — either in-house payroll software or with assistance from their CA or payroll provider.

04

Form 16 issued to employee by June 15

The complete Form 16 (Part A + Part B) is issued to each employee by June 15. Many employers issue it digitally via email or payroll portals.

What if your employer has not issued Form 16?

If June 15 has passed and your employer has not issued Form 16, first send a written request to the HR or payroll team. If the employer is unresponsive, you can still file your ITR using Form 26AS TDS data, your monthly salary slips, and AIS. You may also report non-issuance to the income tax department. Do not delay ITR filing beyond July 31 while waiting for Form 16.

Practical usage

How to Use Form 16 to File Your Income Tax Return

Form 16 contains almost all the data you need to file ITR-1 (Sahaj) or ITR-2 for salaried individuals. Here is how to use each section of Form 16 in your ITR:

Form 16 dataWhere to use in ITR
Gross salary (Part B)Schedule S — Salary income → Gross salary
HRA exemption (Part B)Schedule S → Exempt allowances under Section 10
Standard deduction (Part B)Schedule S → Standard deduction under Section 16
Professional tax (Part B)Schedule S → Entertainment allowance / professional tax
80C deductions (Part B)Schedule VI-A → 80C, 80CCC, 80CCD(1)
80D deductions (Part B)Schedule VI-A → 80D health insurance
Net taxable salary (Part B)Total salary income → Income from salary
TDS amount (Part A)Schedule TDS1 → TDS on salary — amount and TAN

After entering Form 16 data into your ITR, cross-check the TDS amount in Schedule TDS1 against the credit shown in your Form 26AS Part A. Both figures must match before you submit the return. A mismatch at this stage will result in a Section 143(1) demand notice after processing.

Multiple employers in the same year

If you changed jobs during the financial year, you will receive a separate Form 16 from each employer. Combine the salary figures from both Part Bs, claim deductions only once, and include both TDS entries in Schedule TDS1. If you did not inform your new employer about salary from the previous employer, the new employer may not have deducted sufficient TDS — you may need to pay the shortfall as self-assessment tax before filing.

Critical step before filing

How to Reconcile Form 16 with Form 26AS and AIS

Never file your ITR using Form 16 data alone without verifying it against Form 26AS and AIS. Here is the correct reconciliation sequence:

  1. 1

    Download Form 26AS from the income tax portal or TRACES and verify that the TDS amount and employer TAN in Part A of Form 26AS match exactly with what appears in Form 16 Part A.

  2. 2

    If the TDS amount in Form 26AS is lower than what Form 16 shows, your employer may have deducted TDS but not yet deposited it or may have made an error in the TDS return. Contact HR to resolve this before filing.

  3. 3

    Download AIS from the income tax portal and review all listed income sources — salary, interest, dividend, securities. Confirm that salary income aligns with what Form 16 shows.

  4. 4

    If Form 16 shows deductions your employer computed but you want to change — for example, a higher HRA claim or an additional 80C investment not declared to the employer — you can revise these in the ITR directly.

  5. 5

    Compute tax under both old and new regime if needed. If your employer used the old regime but you prefer the new regime, you can switch while filing the ITR — subject to conditions for business income.

  6. 6

    Pay any self-assessment tax shortfall via Challan 280 on the income tax portal and add the challan details in Schedule IT of the ITR before submitting.

Avoid these errors

Common Form 16 Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Accepting Form 16 without verifying it against Form 26AS

Fix: Always cross-check TDS figures in Form 16 Part A with your Form 26AS before filing. Never assume they match.

Filing ITR based on Form 16 alone without considering other income

Fix: Form 16 only covers salary income. Bank interest, rental income, capital gains, freelance income and dividend must be separately added to the ITR.

Missing deductions not declared to the employer

Fix: You can claim additional deductions in your ITR — such as HRA not submitted to HR, 80C investments missed, or 80D premiums not declared — directly when filing, even if they are not in Form 16.

Not accounting for salary from previous employer

Fix: If you changed jobs, collect Form 16 from both employers. Add both salary incomes and both TDS entries. Compute consolidated tax and pay any shortfall as self-assessment tax.

Frequently asked questions

Form 16 FAQs

Q.What is Form 16?

Form 16 is a TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) certificate issued by an employer to a salaried employee under Section 203 of the Income Tax Act, 1961. It certifies that the employer has deducted income tax from the employee's salary and deposited the same with the Central Government. Form 16 is the primary document used by salaried employees to file their Income Tax Return (ITR). It consists of two parts — Part A downloaded from TRACES and Part B prepared by the employer.

Q.What is the difference between Form 16, Form 16A and Form 16B?

Form 16 is a TDS certificate specifically for salary income — issued by employers to employees. Form 16A is a TDS certificate for non-salary income such as bank interest, professional fees, rent received, commission or contractor payments deducted under various sections other than Section 192. Form 16B is a TDS certificate issued by the buyer of immovable property to the seller for TDS deducted under Section 194IA on property purchase transactions above ₹50 lakh.

Q.Is it mandatory for an employer to issue Form 16?

Yes. Under Section 203 of the Income Tax Act, every employer who deducts TDS from an employee's salary is legally required to issue Form 16 to the employee by June 15 of the assessment year — that is, by June 15, 2025 for FY 2024-25. If the employer fails to issue Form 16 within this deadline, a penalty of ₹100 per day may apply under Section 272A(2)(g) until the certificate is issued.

Q.Can I file ITR without Form 16?

Yes, you can file your ITR without Form 16, though it requires more effort. You need your salary slips for all months in the financial year, Form 26AS and AIS to identify TDS credited against your PAN, bank statements for salary credits, and investment proofs for deductions. The income figures from your salary slips and the TDS credits from Form 26AS together give you the data needed to prepare and file the return. Taxvio can assist you with ITR filing even when Form 16 is unavailable.

Q.What should I do if Form 16 has wrong information?

If your Form 16 contains errors — wrong salary figures, missing deductions, incorrect PAN, or wrong HRA calculation — you should immediately contact your employer's HR or payroll department and request a revised Form 16. The employer must correct the TDS return filed with TRACES and issue a fresh Form 16 with the corrected data. Do not file your ITR based on an incorrect Form 16, as mismatches against Form 26AS will trigger a Section 143(1) demand.

Q.How is Form 16 different from payslips?

A payslip shows the salary computation and deductions for a single month. Form 16 is an annual document that consolidates your entire financial year's salary, exemptions, deductions, and tax computation — certified by the employer and verified against TRACES records. Form 16 is a legal tax certificate; payslips are internal payroll documents. For income tax purposes, Form 16 is the authoritative document.

Q.Can I get Form 16 from the income tax portal directly?

Not directly. Only Part A of Form 16 is generated by TRACES and is available to the employer for download. The employer then issues it to you. Part B is prepared by the employer independently. You cannot download your own Form 16 from the income tax portal as an employee. However, you can verify TDS credits on your Form 16 against what appears in your Form 26AS on the income tax portal.
